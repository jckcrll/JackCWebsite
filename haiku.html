jack carlyle

haiku.

snow comes in silence,
settles for a while, then melts -
always calm, always

clouds float overhead,
watching the world through god's eyes,
so weightless, care-free

cold zips touching chins
making clouds in winter air
the smoking garden

serious people
discussing serious things
i prefer nonsense

time goes faster now
summer flew by like a bird
south, for the winter

sparrows swim through sky
tiny jewels cover windows
the world is drowning

digging for treasure
boxes of old love-letters
x marks the spot

cars rocket past
dragging some autumn breeze
down camden road

city reaches up
elevators to the sky
heaven isn't found

the leaves on the trees
flames, traffic cones, sunsets
autumn oranges

tourists take their time
carefully placing each step
on the cold, cold ground

packets of data
fingers to electrons to
colours on a screen
